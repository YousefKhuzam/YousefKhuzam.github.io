The Rise of Sophisticated Scams

Lately, I've noticed a rise in scams targeting professionals and job seekers in the GCC region. These scams have become increasingly sophisticated, using AI and social engineering to appear legitimate. Let's break down how these scams work and how to protect yourself.

How the Scams Work

  • Fake LinkedIn Profiles: The scammers create polished profiles, complete with fake work histories and certifications. They actively post career tips to get people's attention and use AI-modified photos to make their profiles appear more authentic.
  • Trapping Victims: They direct people to bogus educational websites, claiming to be accredited by well-known institutions but it's all fake.
  • Collecting Personal Info: Victims are asked to fill out forms with personal and financial information, which is later used for fraud.
  • Small Payment Requests: Scammers start by asking for small amounts, like application fees or course deposits, which quickly add up.
  • Worthless Certificates: In the end, victims are left with unrecognized certificates that hold no value for career advancement.

Protecting Yourself

Here are some key steps to protect yourself from these scams:

  • Always verify LinkedIn profiles by checking for mutual connections and company affiliations
  • Research educational institutions thoroughly before enrolling
  • Never share personal or financial information without proper verification
  • Be wary of unsolicited offers for quick degrees or certifications
  • Check accreditation status with official educational authorities

What to Do If You're Targeted

If you suspect you're being targeted by a degree mill scam:

  • Report the profile to LinkedIn
  • Document all communications
  • Report to local authorities if you've shared personal information
  • Warn your network about the scam
